Overview

  • What is music technology?
    • Music technology is any device, machine or tool used by musicians and composers to make, record, perform, compose, notate, analyze or edit music.
  • Music technology consists of software and hardware.
  • It falls into 3 catagories: mechanical, electric, and electronic/digital.

Mechanical music technology

Mechanical music technology is any physical device that is used to make music. Some examples include a flute, piano, trumpet and pipe.

Electric music technology

Electric music technology is any instrument that uses electrical circuits. Some examples include the electric guitar, electric piano, and the electric bass. These instruments produce a sound that cannot be heard by the performer and audience unless itâ€™s connected to instrument amplifiers or loudspeakers.

Electronic music technology

Electronic music technology is any device that is used to produce music digitally. Some examples include a computer, and a digital audio workstation (DAW).

Apps for music production

Audacity, Ardour, Tracktion T7, and LMMS are all available on Windows, Mac and Linux. Only Audacity, Ardour and LMMS are open source.

Audacity has a wide range of tools for audio editing and recording. It is user-friendly and supports VST plugins. However only 32-bit plugins are allowed.

Ardour is very user-friendly and like Audacity, it lets you edit and record music. However it is not really beginner-friendly.

Waveform Free (Tracktion T7's successor) has unlimited audio and MIDI tracks. You can host multiple plugin formats, but it doesnâ€™t support many plugins. It gets updated every time Tracktion updates its flagship DAW.

LMMS, like the other apps on this list, lets you record and edit music. It is a cross-platform app and has high quality output. It uses a lot of memory and only supports high-end devices.

Hardware

The Audio Interface connects audio to computers. You can plug it into your microphone and it will send sound signals to your computer.

The USB microphone is used for a lot of things, including recording acoustic instruments, voiceovers, and podcasting. It is portable and cross platform compatible.

The FX processor is for making music effects. It can be portable so you can use it anywhere and any time.
